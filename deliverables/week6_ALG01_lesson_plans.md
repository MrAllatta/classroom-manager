### **MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 23 — Lesson: Multi-Step Equation Review & Error Analysis**

**Objective:** Review solving multi-step linear equations; identify and correct common procedural errors.

**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Standards Alignment:**
- NY 8.EE.C.7a: Solve linear equations with rational coefficients
- MP1: Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them
- MP3: Construct viable arguments

**Instructional Flow:**

| Time | Component | Activity | Materials |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 0–5 min | Warm-Up | *See 2026-09-23_warm_up.pdf* — 3 quick multi-step equations; students solve and identify first step. | Slide deck or printed copy |
| 5–10 min | Debrief & Connection | Review answers; highlight common misstep (e.g., forgetting to distribute or divide all terms). Introduce error-analysis frame. | Whiteboard examples |
| 10–30 min | Main Activity: Error Analysis Task | **Partner activity:** Distribute problem set with 6 worked solutions (some correct, some with deliberate errors). Students classify as "Correct" or "Error," then identify and explain the mistake in sentence form. Circulate; note misconceptions. | *error_analysis_set_UNIT01.pdf* |
| 30–40 min | Gallery Walk & Whole-Class Debrief | Post partner solutions around room. Class walks and marks agreement/disagreement. Reconvene; discuss most common errors and why they occur (procedural vs. conceptual). | Poster paper, markers |
| 40–45 min | Exit Ticket | *See 2026-09-23_exit_ticket.pdf* — Solve one multi-step equation; explain steps. | Printed or digital form |

**Formative Assessment Checkpoints:**
- Warm-up: Can students identify the first step in a multi-step equation?
- Circulating during partner work: Can they identify *where* an error occurred?
- Exit ticket: Can they explain the procedural reasoning?

**Teacher Notes:**
- This lesson bridges the procedural work of weeks 1–5 into the reflective, error-aware mindset needed for the assessment.
- If students struggle with the error-analysis task, switch to a teacher-led fishbowl model (solve together, pause at critical steps, ask "What should come next?").
- Collect exit tickets; use responses to prioritize Thursday's small-group interventions.

### **TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 24 — Lesson: Applications & Word Problems (Contextual Review)**

**Objective:** Translate real-world scenarios into linear equations; solve and interpret solutions in context.

**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Standards Alignment:**
- NY 8.EE.C.7b: Solve word problems leading to linear equations
- MP2: Reason abstractly and quantitatively
- MP4: Model with mathematics

**Instructional Flow:**

| Time | Component | Activity | Materials |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 0–5 min | Warm-Up | *See 2026-09-24_warm_up.pdf* — 2 brief word-problem setup questions: "Write an equation for: A phone plan costs $50 plus $0.10 per minute. Total bill is $75. How many minutes?" | Slide deck or printed copy |
| 5–10 min | Debrief & Frame | Review warm-up; discuss the *translation* process: define variable, identify known/unknown, write equation, solve, check reasonableness. | Anchor chart (optional) |
| 10–30 min | Problem-Solving Stations (Carousel) | Set up 3–4 stations, each with a different multi-step word problem (geometry, age, money, distance). Groups rotate every 5–6 min. At each station, students: (1) define variable, (2) write equation, (3) solve, (4) write a sentence explaining the answer. | Station sheets, printed problems |
| 30–38 min | Whole-Class Synthesis | Bring class together. Have 1–2 groups share solutions from 1–2 stations. Ask: "Why does your answer make sense?" and "What if the constraints changed?" | Whiteboard |
| 38–45 min | Exit Ticket | *See 2026-09-24_exit_ticket.pdf* — One word problem (similar to station work); students write equation, solve, and interpret. | Printed or digital form |

**Formative Assessment Checkpoints:**
- Warm-up: Can students identify what variable to define?
- Station work: Can they *translate* the scenario accurately?
- Exit ticket: Can they solve and write a meaningful interpretation?

**Teacher Notes:**
- Carousel/station work keeps energy high on Tuesday and allows differentiation: easier problems at one station, extension at another.
- Observe students who struggle with the *translation* step; note them for Thursday small-group work.
- Reinforce the phrase "In context, this means…" to anchor the solution to the real-world scenario.
