## Unit Detail

---

### Unit 1 — Foundations: Numbers, Expressions, and the Language of Algebra
**Duration:** 15 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary CCSS-M Standards**
- N-RN.1 — Explain how the definition of rational exponents follows from extending integer exponent properties
- N-RN.2 — Rewrite expressions involving radicals and rational exponents
- N-RN.3 — Explain why sums/products of rationals are rational; that irrational + rational = irrational
- A-SSE.1a — Interpret parts of an expression (terms, factors, coefficients)
- A-SSE.1b — Interpret complicated expressions in context

**Essential Questions**
1. What makes an expression meaningful in a mathematical context?
2. How does the number system extend beyond integers, and why does it matter?
3. How do we communicate mathematical relationships using symbolic language?

### Unit 2 — Equations and Inequalities in One Variable
**Duration:** 18 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary CCSS-M Standards**
- A-CED.1 — Create equations and inequalities in one variable and use them to solve problems
- A-CED.4 — Rearrange formulas to highlight a quantity of interest
- A-REI.1 — Explain each step in solving an equation; construct a reasoned argument
- A-REI.3 — Solve linear equations and inequalities in one variable

**Essential Questions**
1. What does it mean to "solve" an equation, and what justifies each step?
2. How are equations and inequalities different, and how are their solution sets different?
3. How do we model real-world constraints using equations and inequalities?

### Unit 3 — Linear Functions and Their Graphs
**Duration:** 22 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary CCSS-M Standards**
- F-IF.1 — Understand that a function assigns exactly one output to each input; use function notation
- F-IF.2 — Use function notation; evaluate functions for inputs in their domains
- F-IF.4 — Interpret key features of graphs and tables in context (intercepts, intervals, rate of change)
- F-IF.5 — Relate the domain of a function to its graph and context
- F-IF.7a — Graph linear functions; identify intercepts and slope
- A-CED.2 — Create equations in two variables to represent relationships; graph on coordinate axes

**Essential Questions**
1. What is a function, and how is it different from a general relation?
2. What does the graph of a linear function tell us about the relationship it models?
3. How do rate of change and initial value appear in equations, tables, and graphs?

### Unit 8 — Factoring and Solving Quadratic Equations
**Duration:** 20 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary CCSS-M Standards**
- A-SSE.3a — Factor a quadratic expression to reveal the zeros of the function it defines
- A-SSE.3b — Complete the square to reveal the maximum or minimum of a quadratic function
- A-REI.4a — Use completing the square to derive the quadratic formula

### Unit 9 — Quadratic Functions and Their Graphs

- F-IF.7a — Graph quadratic functions; show intercepts, maxima, minima
