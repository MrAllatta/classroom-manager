## Unit Detail

### Unit 1 — Foundations: Numbers, Expressions, and the Language of Algebra
**Duration:** 15 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-N.RN.3** — Rational and irrational numbers: operations, equivalent forms (including rationalizing denominators of the form \(a/\sqrt{b}\)), and categorizing sums/products.
- **AI-A.SSE.1a** — Write the standard form of a polynomial; identify terms, coefficients, degree, leading coefficient, constant term.
- **AI-A.SSE.1b** — Interpret expressions by viewing one or more parts as a single entity (fluency expectation).

**Essential Questions**
1. What makes an expression meaningful in a mathematical context?
2. How does the number system extend beyond integers, and why does it matter?
3. How do we communicate mathematical relationships using symbolic language?

### Unit 2 — Equations and Inequalities in One Variable
**Duration:** 18 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-A.CED.1** — Create equations and inequalities in one variable for a context (linear, quadratic, exponential models; linear inequalities only; no compound inequalities).
- **AI-A.CED.4** — Rearrange formulas to highlight a quantity of interest.
- **AI-A.REI.1a** — Explain each step when solving linear and quadratic equations; justify the method.
- **AI-A.REI.3** — Solve linear equations and inequalities in one variable (no compound inequalities in Algebra I).

**Essential Questions**
1. What does it mean to "solve" an equation, and what justifies each step?
2. How are equations and inequalities different, and how are their solution sets different?
3. How do we model real-world constraints using equations and inequalities?

**Primary standards**

- **AI-F.IF.4a** — Interpret key features of graphs/tables; sketch from verbal descriptions (linear, quadratic, square root, piecewise, exponential \(a(b)^x\) as per course limits).

**Essential Questions**
1. What is a function, and how is it different from a general relation?
2. What does the graph of a linear function tell us about the relationship it models?
3. How do rate of change and initial value appear in equations, tables, and graphs?

### Unit 7 — Polynomial Expressions and Operations
**Duration:** 14 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-A.SSE.2** — Use structure to rewrite expressions (Algebra I: GCF, difference of squares, trinomials \(ax^2+bx+c\) with \(a=1\), combinations; no grouping or sum/difference of cubes).
- **AI-A.APR.1** — Add, subtract, multiply polynomials (fluency recommendation).
- **AI-A.APR.3** — Identify zeros when factorizations are available (quadratics; cubics with given factors).

**Essential Questions**
1. How are polynomials similar to and different from integers as mathematical objects?
2. What does the structure of a polynomial expression reveal about its behavior?
3. How do operations on polynomials prepare us for solving quadratic equations?

### Unit 8 — Factoring and Solving Quadratic Equations
**Duration:** 20 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-A.SSE.2** — Structural rewriting to support solving (factoring limitations per Algebra I).
- **AI-A.REI.4a** — Completing the square with lead coefficient 1 and even linear term; connect to quadratic formula (derive exposure, not full derivation requirement).
- **AI-A.REI.4b** — Solve quadratics by inspection, square roots, factoring, completing the square, formula, and graphing; simplest radical form; discriminant for no real solutions.

**Essential Questions**
1. Why do quadratic equations sometimes have two solutions, one solution, or no real solutions?
2. How do different solution methods (factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula) relate to each other?
3. What does it mean to "factor" an expression, and what do the factors reveal?

### Unit 9 — Quadratic Functions and Their Graphs
**Duration:** 18 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-F.IF.4a** — Key features of quadratics in context (zeros, intercepts, intervals, max/min, symmetry).
- **AI-F.IF.5** — Domain from graph and context.
- **AI-F.IF.7a** — Graph quadratics (and linear/exponential per course) showing key features.
- **AI-F.IF.7b** — Graph square root and piecewise-defined (step, absolute value) functions; key features.
- **AI-F.IF.8a** — Equivalent forms of quadratics to show zeros, extrema, symmetry; interpret in context.
- **AI-F.BF.3a** — Transformations \(f(x)+k\), \(kf(x)\), \(f(x+k)\); find \(k\) from graphs; write new function (linear, quadratic, square root, absolute value, exponential per limits).
- **AI-A.CED.1** — One-variable quadratic models from context.

**Essential Questions**
1. How does the vertex form of a quadratic reveal information that standard form does not?
2. What do transformations of a quadratic function look like graphically and algebraically?
3. How do we choose the right form of a quadratic equation for a given purpose?

### Unit 10 — Statistics: Describing and Interpreting Data
**Duration:** 13 instructional days  
**End-of-Unit Test:** Yes

**Primary standards**
- **AI-S.ID.1** — Dot plots, histograms, box plots.
- **AI-S.ID.2** — Compare center and spread (including sample standard deviation, \(n-1\) denominator, inference to population).
- **AI-S.ID.3** — Shape, center, spread, outliers.
- **AI-S.ID.5** — Two-way tables; joint, marginal, conditional relative frequencies.
- **AI-S.ID.6** / **AI-S.ID.6a** — Scatter plots; fit functions to data (Algebra I emphasis on linear models; calculator regression).
- **AI-S.ID.7** — Interpret slope and intercept of a linear model.
- **AI-S.ID.8** — Correlation coefficient (technology).
- **AI-S.ID.9** — Correlation vs causation.

**Essential Questions**
1. How do we choose the right summary statistics and displays to describe a data set?
2. What does a linear model tell us, and how do we evaluate how well it fits?
3. Why is the distinction between correlation and causation important in real-world reasoning?
