# classroom-manager
This project oversees the work of a professional k-12 educator. This multi-faceted role has never fit into human scope, and has expanded as society attempts to solve every problem with this single underresourced human role. On one level a teacher of children is accountable for their safety. They know whether a student is under their supervision and report this accordingly. Beyond attendance, which is itself a signifant responsibility, the teacher is also responsible for enrichment, documentation, planning, assessment, communication, reporting, counseling, case-management, collaboration, and intellectual rigor. In reality the role of any single teacher is that of a team.
# the work
Teaching means planning, writing lesson plans from researched curriculum, producing and packaging files.
Teaching means implementing, bringing learning resources (materials) into a physical space with human bodies (because their parents need to work!) and engaging the students in those resources in real time.
Teaching means communicating expectations with students, families, administrators, and colleagues. Communication is often through email, but also Remind, phone, text, or school-based system. Communication with students in-person is part of implementation, and can happen at different times throughout the physical school day, but also includes written communication with the student.
Reporting through gradebook software, commenting, documenting interactions, entering data from classroom observations and hand graded paper and pencil work. 
Scheduling is central to a teacher's day. Most of the day is scheduled with physical class meetings. There are also scheduled events after school, meetings, scheduled calls, clubs, professional learning and workshops, and professional coordination.
Assessing students means generating appropriate curriculum-aligned materials and producing actionable results to all stakeholders in reasonable time, i.e. within time when the data is relevant and can be used to affect corrective action.
Teaching is an on-going process of nudging through data and communication to instigate small corrective actions toward established goals and metrics. The loop of nudges must be tight to be effective.
# the team
A teacher must have a team to produce quality outcomes in every domain they oversee with sustainable human resource allocation. Current role division largely follows the system they interact with, gradebook, calendar, which is reasonable for tool based agents.
## scheduler
oversees the google cacan use a tool connected to the teacher's google calendar. creates events and includes contacts based on network and student graph. 
## messager
oversees messages of all types, including emails, comments, scripts for voice call. Not focused on student work feedback but on moving forward communication loops which tend to be about student data consistency and significance.
## assessor
Take the NY Algebra Regents. It is connected to a set of standards and assesses those standards on its cumulative exam at some proportion. The most aligned assessment strategy would weigh standards in course assessment at the same proportion. The assessor maintains the table of assessed standards evidencde and evaluation. The assessor has database tools that allow them to report, frame, and flag student assessment data. The assessor reports on student learning.
## researcher
new knowledge is brought in by the researcher. questions about aligning practices and concepts to rigorous professional standards.
## curriculum
writes and adapts scope and sequence in collaboration with existing and well researchered curricula.
